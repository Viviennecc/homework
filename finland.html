The country experiences four distinct seasons: snowy winters (talvi) ideal for the Northern Lights, a blossoming spring (kevät), lush summers (kesä) with the "Midnight Sun," and a colorful autumn (syksy) known as ruska Visit Finland. Currency and Consumption

Finland uses the Euro (€) as its official currency. It is a highly digitalized, near-cashless society where debit and credit cards (especially Visa and Mastercard) are accepted almost everywhere, from high-end restaurants to remote market stalls. Consumption in Finland is relatively expensive compared to other European nations. In 2026, expect to pay approximately €4–€5 for a regular coffee and €11–€15 for a "lounas" (lunch) buffet, which is a popular local way to save on dining costs. Dinner mains typically range from €25 to over €50. Tipping is not expected, as service charges are legally included in prices.

Helsinki

Helsinki Cathedral

Helsinki Cathedral, or Helsingin tuomiokirkko, is the city's most iconic landmark. Towering over Senate Square, this neoclassical masterpiece was designed by architect Carl Ludvig Engel and completed in 1852. Its brilliant white façade, striking green domes, and twelve zinc statues of the Apostles create a majestic silhouette against the Finnish sky. The interior is surprisingly minimalist, reflecting traditional Lutheran simplicity. As a functioning church and a primary tourist attraction, it hosts both quiet worship and grand concerts. Climbing its steep stairs offers a popular vantage point for photos of the capital’s vibrant harbour and city centre.

Suomenlinna

Suomenlinna, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a magnificent 18th-century sea fortress built across six islands off the coast of Helsinki. Originally constructed by Sweden as "Sveaborg" to defend against Russian expansion, it now serves as a living district home to 800 residents Suomenlinna Official Site. Visitors can explore well-preserved stone walls, artillery batteries, and mysterious tunnels. Key highlights include the King’s Gate and the Suomenlinna Museum, which details its military history under Swedish, Russian, and Finnish rule. Accessible via a short ferry ride from Market Square, it is a premier destination for history buffs and picnickers alike Visit Helsinki.

Tampere

Moomin Museum

Located in Tampere Hall, the Moomin Museum is the world’s only museum dedicated to the beloved characters created by Tove Jansson. It features an experiential collection of over 2,000 original illustrations and sketches donated by the artist. The centerpiece is a magnificent five-storey, two-metre-tall Moominhouse, meticulously crafted with intricate internal details. Visitors can explore 3D tableaux, interactive exhibits, and a multimedia "Moominvalley". In late 2026, the museum will host the "Moomin Mug Mania" exhibition, exploring the cultural history of these iconic collectibles. It is a contemplative, magical space for fans of all ages.

Pyynikki Observation Tower

The Pyynikki Observation Tower, standing 26 metres tall atop the world’s highest longitudinal gravel ridge, offers the most breathtaking views in Tampere [1, 5]. From the top, visitors enjoy a 360-degree panorama of the shimmering lakes Näsijärvi and Pyhäjärvi. Built in 1929 from local red granite, the tower is equally famous for its ground-floor café, which serves legendary cardamom doughnuts (munkki) [3, 4]. In 2026, it remains a "must-visit" for the perfect blend of natural scenery and local treats. The surrounding nature reserve also features beautiful forest trails ideal for seasonal hiking.

Turku

Turku Castle

Established in 1280, Turku Castle is one of Finland’s oldest and most significant historical monuments Turun linna. This massive stone fortress has served as a defensive bastion, a majestic royal palace, and a prison throughout its long history. Today, it functions as a museum where visitors can wander through medieval rooms, Renaissance banquet halls, and the iconic "King’s Hall." Located at the mouth of the Aura River, the castle provides a deep dive into the era of Swedish rule and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts exploring Finland’s medieval roots Visit Turku.

Turku Cathedral

Considered the "National Sanctuary of Finland," Turku Cathedral was consecrated in 1300 and remains the most important religious building in the country Turun tuomiokirkko. This majestic brick cathedral, located in the heart of the city, is dedicated to the Virgin Mary and St. Henry. Its impressive 101-metre-high tower is a defining feature of the skyline. Inside, the cathedral serves as a museum and final resting place for Swedish Queen Karin Månsdotter and several influential bishops. It is a living monument of Finnish history, hosting regular services and frequent organ concerts Visit Finland.

Rovaniemi

Santa Claus Village

Santa Claus Village is a magical destination where the spirit of Christmas lives year-round. Located exactly on the Arctic Circle, visitors can officially cross the line into the Arctic and receive a certificate for the feat. The main highlight is meeting Santa Claus in his office, followed by sending postcards with a unique Arctic Circle postmark from the Santa Claus Main Post Office. The village also features reindeer sleigh rides, husky parks, and charming boutiques, making it the premier destination for families and holiday enthusiasts worldwide Visit Rovaniemi.

Arktikum

Arktikum is a world-class museum and science centre that provides a deep dive into Arctic life and history. Its most striking feature is the 172-metre-long glass tunnel that extends towards the Ounasjoki River, serving as a "Gateway to the North." Inside, the exhibitions explore the culture of the Sámi people, Arctic nature, and the history of Lapland. It is also one of the best indoor spots to learn about the science behind the Northern Lights through immersive displays, offering a thought-provoking perspective on the changing northern environment Arctic Centre.
